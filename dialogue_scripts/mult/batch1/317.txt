Dr. Reynolds: There's always a balance to be struck. However, considering the potential severity of untreated aneurysms, early detection could save lives and reduce long-term healthcare costs.

Mr. Harper: I see. But how do you address the disagreement over the statistical significance of decreased DBP as a predictor?

Dr. Reynolds: It's an ongoing debate. The policy reflects the current consensus but remains open to revision as more data becomes available.

Mr. Harper: So, it's adaptable?

Dr. Reynolds: Precisely. We're committed to evidence-based practice, and updates will follow as our understanding evolves.
