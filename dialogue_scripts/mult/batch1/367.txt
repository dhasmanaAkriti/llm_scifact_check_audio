Speaker 2: Thank you for sharing this, Dr. Reynolds. While the data is indeed compelling, we must consider all aspects before implementing such a protocol across the board. Can you elaborate on how early screening makes such a difference?

Speaker 2: It's intriguing, but how do we ensure the accuracy of early screening? Misdiagnosis could lead to unnecessary interventions, after all.

Speaker 2: Dr. Reynolds, while I see the merit, policy changes require unanimous support. Do you foresee any resistance from our colleagues?

Speaker 1: Change always meets resistance, Dr. Lee. However, with transparent communication and evidence-based presentations, I believe we can address their concerns. The ultimate goal is to save lives, and this approach offers us that opportunity.

Speaker 2: I agree, saving lives is paramount. Let's organize a meeting with the board to discuss this further. If the evidence holds, we should indeed consider adopting early PDA screening.
